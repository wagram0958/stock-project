# Earnings Watchlist

This file tracks companies that should be monitored through the project's
forensic earnings and conference call analysis mode.

The goal is not to collect every headline. The goal is to track companies where
financial statements, monthly revenue, major announcements, investor materials,
and management language may reveal underpriced opportunities or hidden risks.

## 6226 光鼎 Research Checklist

### Core Questions

- Is recent price momentum supported by improving fundamentals, or mostly by
  short-term market attention?
- Are revenue trends improving in a sustainable way, or driven by temporary
  pull-ins, product mix, or one-off demand?
- Is gross margin improving because of pricing, utilization, product mix, or
  cost control?
- Are operating expenses structurally too heavy relative to revenue scale?
- Is operating cash flow consistent with reported earnings?
- Are inventory, receivables, or payables showing early warning signs?
- Does management provide clear demand visibility, or rely on vague optimism?

### Data to Monitor

- Monthly revenue and year-over-year growth
- Quarterly revenue, gross margin, operating margin, and EPS
- Operating cash flow and free cash flow
- Inventory and receivables turnover
- Major announcements and attention-trading disclosures
- Investor presentation, conference call, or shareholder meeting materials
- Customer, product, and market-mix commentary

### Forensic Angle

Use `AGENTS.md` Earnings and Conference Call Forensic Mode when analyzing this
company. Focus on:

- Hidden risks management may be downplaying
- Whether operating losses are narrowing for structural or temporary reasons
- Whether market expectations are running ahead of confirmed fundamentals
- Any underpriced optionality from LED, optoelectronics, display, automotive,
  industrial, or specialty lighting demand

## Source Priority

1. MOPS public filings and major announcements
2. TWSE OpenAPI data
3. Company IR website and investor materials
4. Financial data platforms for cross-checking
5. Industry news only when it changes the investment thesis

## Output Format

When new data is found, write findings as a buy-side memo:

1. Conclusion
2. Key numbers that matter
3. Hidden or under-discussed risks
4. Underpriced opportunities
5. Management language audit
6. What to verify next
7. Investment implication
