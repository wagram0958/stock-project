# Supply Chain Map

This file stores durable supply-chain and industry-framework notes for Taiwan equity research. It should not collect daily noise. Add only themes that change how companies should be mapped, screened, or compared.

## Durable Themes

### AI Infrastructure Demand

**Status:** Active / durable trend

**Why it matters:**
AI demand is still showing up in Taiwan supply-chain data. Hon Hai reported strong second-quarter and June revenue growth driven by AI-related cloud and networking products, while Reuters also reported that ASE is expanding capacity to support AI demand. These are not just headline themes; they point to continued demand for AI servers, networking, advanced packaging, testing, power, thermal, connector, and system-integration capacity.

**Taiwan supply-chain nodes to map:**

| Node | Taiwan examples to monitor | Key indicators |
|---|---|---|
| AI server assembly / system integration | 2317 Hon Hai and server ODM ecosystem | Monthly revenue, AI server backlog, cloud/networking segment commentary |
| Advanced packaging and testing | 3711 ASE-related ecosystem | Capacity expansion, utilization, capex, margin mix |
| Power / thermal / connector / cable | Power and component suppliers | Revenue acceleration, gross margin stability, customer concentration |
| Networking / optical / optoelectronic components | Optical and LED/opto names, including watchlist names where relevant | Whether revenue is tied to real AI infrastructure demand or only market narrative |

**Research rule:**
Treat broad AI headlines as a theme filter, not a buy signal. A company must show at least two of the following before being promoted from "theme exposure" to "fundamental exposure": revenue acceleration, margin improvement, operating leverage, order visibility, named product/customer evidence, or cash-flow confirmation.

### Robotics / Automation

**Status:** Active but evidence quality varies by company

**Why it matters:**
Robotics and automation are likely to remain important investment narratives, but many Taiwan names are exposed indirectly through components, controllers, machine vision, motors, reducers, industrial PCs, sensors, power modules, or automation equipment rather than complete robots.

**Research rule:**
Do not classify a company as a robotics beneficiary only because news or market discussion uses the word "robotics." Require a concrete supply-chain role and measurable business evidence.
